About This Stereo-Barrier Hologram

This implementation uses a parallax barrier technique to create a glasses-free 3D illusion that literally jumps out of the screen. The effect works on any device—phones, laptops, tablets—without requiring special hardware or glasses.

  • Stereo-barrier display: Uses left/right eye separation with a parallax mask
  • 4cm pop-out effect: Object jumps 40mm out of the screen during animation
  • Motion parallax: Follows mouse movement and phone gyroscope
  • 60 FPS performance: Optimized for smooth animations on mid-range devices
  • No hardware required: Pure WebGL + optical trickery

How It Works

The parallax barrier uses alternating slits (1 pixel white, 1 pixel black) to separate the left and right eye views. When you move your head or device, each eye sees a slightly different perspective, creating the 3D depth illusion. The object appears to float in front of the screen!
